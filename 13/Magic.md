# The Nature of Magic
Magic is among the oldest and least understood forces in existence. Entire civilizations have risen upon its mastery, while others have been destroyed attempting to uncover its deepest truths. Though countless theories exist concerning its origins and operation, most learned traditions agree upon one principle: magic is not a single force, but a structure underlying reality itself.

The most widely accepted cosmological model among sages, priests, mystics, and arcane scholars describes magic as a vast metaphysical tapestry known simply as **the Weave**.
## The Weave
According to this model, existence is formed through the interaction of two great structures: the **warp** and the **weft**.

The **warp** is believed to emanate from the Outer Planes and is associated most strongly with the ordering principles of existence: meaning, identity, morality, spiritual authority, destiny, and divine structure. It gives **shape** to reality, defining the metaphysical laws and higher patterns by which the multiverse is ordered.

The **weft**, by contrast, is thought to radiate from the Inner Planes and governs substance, energy, transformation, motion, and elemental expression. It gives **substance** to reality, forming the material and energetic framework through which existence manifests.

Where warp and weft intersect, the fabric of reality takes form.

Yet the Weave is not composed solely of its threads. Between the crossings of warp and weft lie the spaces between structure and substance: the unseen interstices from which coherence itself emerges. These spaces are sometimes called **Essence**.

Unlike the warp and weft, which are believed to arise from the planar poles of existence, Essence appears to emerge naturally from the Prime Material and from living existence itself. Essence provides **cohesion**, binding shape and substance together where they meet and allowing individual beings to exist as distinct, unified selves within the larger structure of reality.

Though subtle, Essence is no less fundamental than the threads surrounding it. Many sages argue that without it, neither spirit nor matter could maintain continuity for even a moment.
## The Three Great Magical Traditions
Most forms of supernatural power are understood as interactions with one or more aspects of the Weave.
#### Arcane Magic
Wizards, bards, and other arcane practitioners most commonly manipulate the **weft** of the Weave. Through study, formulae, symbolic systems, performance, experimentation, and technical discipline, they shape the energetic and material structures underlying reality.

Arcane magic is therefore associated with:

- transformation,
- elemental force,
- perception,
- motion,
- energy,
- and the manipulation of material substance.

Unlike divine practitioners, arcane casters do not rely upon spiritual authority, but upon learned methods capable of producing predictable interactions with the Weave.
#### Divine Magic
Priests, druids, shamans, and other servants of higher powers most commonly manipulate the **warp** of the Weave. Through devotion, alignment, sacred authority, ritual obligation, or spiritual attunement, they invoke the ordering principles that give shape to reality.

Divine magic is therefore associated with:

- faith,
- cosmic order,
- spiritual law,
- morality,
- fate,
- and metaphysical authority.

Though divine casters may channel elemental force or miraculous power, they do so through alignment with higher structures rather than through direct manipulation of substance itself.
#### Mysticism
Mystics, seers, and practitioners of inner disciplines focus not upon the threads themselves, but upon the **Essence** that exists between them.

Rather than invoking external powers or shaping arcane substance directly, mystics cultivate mastery over their own internal cohesion, exerting will and perception upon the subtle tensions and spaces from which consciousness, identity, and supernatural potential emerge.

Mysticism is therefore associated with:

- inner discipline,
- consciousness,
- perception,
- will,
- self-mastery,
- and the latent supernatural potential inherent in living beings.

The manipulation of Essence is far rarer than either divine or arcane magic and is often poorly understood even by accomplished spellcasters.
## Innate Magicks
Not all supernatural abilities arise from formal magical training.

Many creatures possess abilities that are magical by nature rather than by study, prayer, or discipline. Dragons breathe flame, medusae petrify with a glance, celestials radiate supernatural presence, and elementals embody the raw forces of their native planes.

Such powers are commonly referred to as **innate magicks**.

Most cosmological traditions explain these abilities as natural expressions of Essence: supernatural manifestations arising from the intrinsic cohesion of the creature itself rather than from externally prepared spell structures.

This shared origin explains why innate magical abilities often resemble spells while functioning according to entirely different rules.

Innate magicks:

- do not normally require memorization,
- do not rely upon spell components,
- and are resolved according to the creature’s own abilities rather than the rules governing arcane, divine, or mystical practitioners.

## The Limits of Understanding
Despite centuries of study, no mortal tradition fully comprehends the true nature of the Weave.

Priests dispute the primacy of divine structure. Wizards argue over competing theories of arcane causality. Mystics claim that both misunderstand the spaces between. Philosophers, alchemists, elementalists, and countless other traditions all describe the same underlying phenomena through radically different conceptual frameworks.

These contradictions do not necessarily invalidate one another.

The Weave is vast enough that many systems of understanding may function simultaneously while still remaining incomplete.

For most practitioners, perfect understanding is less important than reliable practice. A spell properly cast still functions whether the caster understands the ultimate truth of the cosmos or not.

And perhaps that is the greatest mystery of all.

## Arcane Magic
Arcane magic is the disciplined manipulation of the weft of the Weave: the underlying structures of substance, energy, transformation, and material expression that give form to the multiverse. Unlike divine magic, which invokes higher ordering principles through the warp, arcane magic relies upon learned techniques, symbolic formulae, trained perception, and deliberate acts of will to shape magical forces directly.

To the untrained observer, arcane spellcasting may appear mysterious or irrational, but to practitioners it is a structured discipline governed by repeatable principles and accumulated knowledge. Though no mortal tradition fully understands the true nature of the Weave, centuries of experimentation and scholarship have produced reliable methods by which arcane effects may be studied, classified, reproduced, and taught.

These systems of classification do not describe separate sources of magical power. Rather, they represent different methods by which arcane practitioners organize and understand manipulation of the weft.

Some traditions classify magic according to underlying principles and methodologies. Others emphasize observable results or energetic manifestations. Still others focus upon the practical techniques through which spells are physically executed.

Because these systems describe different aspects of magical practice, a single spell may belong to several categories simultaneously.

A spell such as _fireball_, for example, might be classified:

- as Invocation/Evocation according to the philosophical traditions,
- as Fire according to the effectual traditions,
- and as Song or Alchemy according to the thaumaturgical traditions, depending upon how it is cast.

These classifications are not contradictory. Each describes a different aspect of the spell’s structure or execution.

Likewise, these systems are not limited exclusively to arcane spellcasters. Priests, druids, and other wielders of divine magic invoke powers through different means, yet the resulting spells may still be described using many of the same classifications, because these traditions describe magical structure rather than magical origin. A priestly healing spell may be considered necromantic in philosophical structure, while a divine _flame strike_ may also be classified as a fire effect or invocation.

The classifications presented here therefore describe the structure and behavior of magic itself, rather than the social role or power source of the caster.
### Universal Magic
Before a wizard may specialize in any advanced form of arcane practice, he must first master the fundamentals collectively known as **Universal Magic**.

Universal Magic encompasses the foundational principles common to nearly all arcane traditions:

- detection and interpretation of magical phenomena,
- comprehension of symbolic formulae,
- basic manipulation of arcane structure,
- and the safe handling of magical energies.

Without this foundation, further study is impossible.

Universal spells are therefore available to all wizard traditions regardless of specialization. They form the common language of arcane practice and are often referred to informally as “the gateway arts” or “Sorcery 101” among learned practitioners.
### Philosophical Traditions

The oldest and most widespread system of arcane classification organizes spells according to shared underlying principles and methods of operation. These classifications are known collectively as the **Philosophical Traditions**.

The philosophical traditions form the basis of most formal magical education and are especially associated with academies, colleges, libraries, and institutional traditions of wizardry.

Though the effects produced by spells within a school may vary greatly, all share certain structural principles in how they manipulate the Weave.
#### The School of Abjuration
The School of Abjuration concerns protective spells that ward, banish, contain, suppress, or negate harmful forces, creatures, and magical effects.
#### The School of Conjuration/Summoning
The School of Conjuration and Summoning governs spells that transport creatures, matter, energies, or forces from one location or plane to another.
#### The School of Divination
The School of Divination encompasses spells that reveal information otherwise hidden, distant, obscured, forgotten, or yet to come.
#### The School of Enchantment/Charm

The School of Enchantment and Charm governs spells that alter qualities, perceptions, emotions, thoughts, or behavior in creatures and objects.
#### The School of Illusion/Phantasm
The School of Illusion and Phantasm concerns spells that deceive the senses or mind through false, distorted, or quasi-real perceptions.
#### The School of Invocation/Evocation
The School of Invocation and Evocation channels magical energy into direct manifestations of force, power, or created substance.
#### The School of Necromancy
The School of Necromancy governs the manipulation of life force, death, vitality, decay, and the boundary between living and dead states.
#### The School of Transmutation
The School of Transmutation concerns spells that alter the physical properties, form, composition, or state of creatures, objects, and energies.
### Effectual Traditions
While the philosophical traditions classify spells according to method and principle, the effectual traditions organize magic according to common manifestations, energetic expressions, or observable results.

These traditions are less associated with formal academies and more often transmitted through apprenticeship, lineage, or practical specialization.

Spells grouped within a school of effect may belong to many different philosophical schools while still producing related manifestations.
#### The School of Elementalism
The School of Elementalism studies the manipulation of the fundamental elemental substances and energies associated with the Inner Planes.

The School of Elementalism is further subdivided into the four elemental branches:

- **Air** — Air effects govern wind, atmosphere, flight, breath, gases, storms, and aerial movement.
- **Earth** — Earth effects govern stone, soil, metal, minerals, stability, and geological structure.
- **Fire** — Fire effects govern heat, flame, combustion, radiance, and energetic release.
- **Water** — Water effects govern liquid, cold, ice, fluid motion, pressure, and adaptive flow.
#### The School of Dimensional Magic
The School of Dimensional Magic governs spells that manipulate distance, spatial relation, planar boundaries, extradimensional structures, and movement between locations or realities.
#### The School of Force
The School of Force focuses upon the creation and manipulation of coherent magical energy independent of elemental substance, often manifesting as invisible pressure, barriers, impacts, or structured arcane constructs.
#### The School of Shadow
The School of Shadow teaches manipulation of darkness, shadow, partial unreality, obscured perception, and energies associated with the Plane of Shadow or similar transitory states.
### Thaumaturgical Traditions
Where the philosophical traditions classify magical principle and the effectual traditions classify magical result, the thaumaturgical traditions classify spells according to the methods by which they are executed.

These traditions focus upon the practical techniques used to shape, stabilize, and release arcane energy.

Thaumaturgical traditions are frequently associated with guilds, craft lineages, ritual societies, performers, and specialized magical trades.
#### The School of Alchemy
The School of Alchemy employs reagents, powders, elixirs, catalysts, and material transformations as the primary means of producing magical effects.
#### The School of Artifice
The School of Artifice channels arcane power through crafted devices, implements, mechanisms, and constructed magical instruments.
#### The School of Geometry
The School of Geometry manipulates magical forces through symbols, diagrams, sigils, measured forms, spatial relationships, and geometric patterns.
#### The School of Song
The School of Song shapes magical energy through voice, resonance, rhythm, harmony, and spoken or sung performance.
#### The School of Wild Magic
The School of Wild Magic manipulates unstable or poorly controlled arcane forces, embracing unpredictability, fluctuation, and chaotic magical interaction as part of the casting process itself.

While many wild magic spells may still be described through other philosophical or effectual classifications, their execution often resists conventional arcane theory. Other spells are unique to wild magic traditions entirely and cannot be reliably reproduced through more stable methodologies.
### Spellbooks and Arcane Formulae
All arcane practitioners rely upon some form of recorded magical knowledge. Whether maintained as a traditional spellbook, coded grimoire, scroll archive, engraved tablets, alchemical ledger, geometric folio, song codex, mnemonic tapestry, or other specialized medium, these repositories contain the formulae through which arcane energies may be understood, stabilized, and reproduced.

To the uninitiated, such texts often appear incomprehensible: dense with symbolic notation, diagrams, obscure references, numerical relationships, cryptic annotations, and seemingly irrational sequences of words or gestures. Yet to a trained practitioner, these markings represent carefully structured methods for shaping the weft of the Weave into repeatable magical effects.

An arcane spell is not merely a collection of spoken words and physical motions. It is a precisely organized energetic structure requiring exact symbolic relationships, mental conditioning, and controlled execution. The spellbook serves as both instructional text and mnemonic framework, allowing the caster to study, internalize, and reproduce these patterns safely.

Because no two arcane traditions interpret magical structure in precisely the same way, spellbooks vary enormously in appearance and organization.

A wizard trained within the philosophical traditions may maintain densely annotated theoretical tomes organized according to metaphysical principles and categorical schools. An elementalist of the effectual traditions may record experiential observations concerning energetic manifestation, environmental conditions, and elemental resonance. A practitioner of the thaumaturgical traditions may instead preserve procedural formulae focused upon reagents, harmonic structures, geometric precision, or crafted devices.

Even among practitioners of the same tradition, notation systems are highly individualized. Experienced wizards develop personal symbolic shorthand, private mnemonic structures, coded references, and theoretical assumptions that may render their spellbooks difficult or dangerous for others to interpret without careful study.

For this reason, possession of a spellbook does not automatically grant understanding of its contents.

Before a spell may be used reliably, the caster must first decipher the formula, comprehend its underlying structure, and adapt the notation into a form compatible with his own understanding and casting methods. This process is represented through the normal rules governing spell acquisition and learning.

Spellbooks are therefore among the most valuable possessions an arcane practitioner can own. Beyond their monetary worth, they represent accumulated years—or even decades—of experimentation, refinement, research, and personal insight. Many wizards protect their spellbooks obsessively through mundane precautions, magical wards, hidden codes, trapped pages, secret compartments, or elaborate concealment.

While it is expensive and time-consuming, a wizard who has the means is well-advised to record important spells in multiple spellbooks. The loss or destruction of a spellbook can cripple a wizard’s ability to prepare and expand his magical repertoire. While previously mastered spells may still remain partially familiar to memory, the caster loses access to the formulae necessary to safely study, reorganize, and stabilize arcane structures for future casting. 

The base cost of a standard spellbook is **50 gp per page**, while a more compact travelling spellbook is **100 gp per page**. These costs include high quality binding that avoids pages falling out, treatments to resist water and pest damage, basic leather covers, as well as the high quality inks used for recording spells. Covers, bindings, and embellishments made from rare or exotic materials may increase this cost substantially at the DM’s discretion. 

A standard spellbook can contain up to **100 pages**, while a travelling spellbook is limited to **50 pages** due to its reinforced portability and compact construction. A mundane scroll used as a spellbook can be equivalent to a spellbook of up to **25 pages** at most.
#### The Structure of Arcane Formulae
Although arcane traditions differ greatly in theory and execution, most spells incorporate several common elements:

- symbolic formulae that define the spell’s energetic structure,
- mnemonic patterns used to stabilize the spell within the caster’s mind,
- procedural instructions governing timing and execution,
- and specific correspondences linking the spell to broader metaphysical principles.

The familiar verbal, somatic, and material components used during spellcasting are merely the outward expressions of these deeper structures. The true complexity of arcane magic lies not in speaking words or making gestures, but in arranging the underlying patterns correctly.

Minor errors in sequence, emphasis, symbolic alignment, or energetic control may disrupt the spell entirely or produce dangerous unintended consequences.

As a result, arcane study demands exceptional discipline, concentration, and intellectual rigor. Even gifted practitioners must spend years mastering the symbolic languages, mathematical relationships, philosophical frameworks, and mental exercises necessary to manipulate arcane energies consistently.
#### Personal Formulae and Arcane Variation
No spell exists as a single universally agreed-upon formula.

While many spells share common names and recognizable effects across cultures and traditions, the actual methods used to cast them may differ dramatically between practitioners. One wizard’s _fireball_ may rely upon geometric compression of elemental fire energies, while another invokes complex chains of transformational release, harmonic resonance, or alchemical ignition.

These differing approaches are not necessarily incompatible. Rather, they represent alternative methods of achieving similar manipulations of the weft.

This variation explains why spellbooks cannot simply be exchanged and read without effort, why magical traditions produce competing theories concerning the same phenomena, and why some arcane practitioners become fiercely protective or dismissive of rival methodologies.

It also explains why advanced magical research remains possible. Arcane magic is not static knowledge, but an evolving body of practice shaped by discovery, experimentation, and individual insight.
### Learning and Recording Spells
Arcane spells are not gained automatically through advancement. A wizard must acquire new spells through study, instruction, research, trade, discovery, or theft before they may be used.

A wizard may encounter new spells in many forms, including:

- another caster’s spellbook,
- scrolls and magical texts,
- academy libraries,
- guild archives,
- ancient ruins,
- captured notes,
- or original magical research.

Before a newly acquired spell may be used, the wizard must first learn and record it within his own spellbook.
#### Learning Spells
When a wizard encounters a spell not already known - if it is of a level he is capable of casting - he may attempt to learn it through study and analysis. Learning spells categorized as Universal Magic is automatically successful, for all other spells success is determined by rolling against the character’s **Chance to Learn Spell** based upon their Intelligence ability score, and modified by any specialist or other bonuses or penalties. 

A successful check indicates that the wizard successfully comprehends the spell’s structure and may record it in his spellbook.

Failure indicates that the wizard cannot presently understand the spell well enough to master it. Additional attempts may be permitted only if the character gains a point of Intelligence, advances in level, receives instruction from another caster, or substantially alters the circumstances of study, at the DM’s discretion.
##### Deciphering Arcane Writings
Spellbooks, scrolls, and magical writings produced by unfamiliar traditions are often more difficult to interpret.

A wizard attempting to identify or understand unknown magical notation may require:

- _read magic_,
- proficiency checks,
- Intelligence checks,
- or extended study,  
    depending upon the complexity of the material and the nature of the writing.

Particularly obscure traditions, encoded texts, alien formulae, or damaged magical writings may impose penalties or require magical assistance before they can be studied safely.
#### Recording Spells
Once a spell is successfully learned, the wizard may record it in his spellbook.

Recording a spell requires:

- access to the source formula,
- sufficient space in the wizard’s own spellbook,
- and uninterrupted study time.

Each spell recorded requires a number of blank spellbook pages equal to the spell's level + 1d6-1.

A recorded spell becomes part of the wizard’s arcane archive and may thereafter be used according to the normal rules of arcane spellcasting..

Spells recorded in another wizard’s notation cannot ordinarily be used directly without first being learned and transcribed into a usable personal format.
##### Spell Exchange and Magical Secrecy
In some regions or traditions, spells are freely traded among allied wizards and scholarly institutions. In others, magical knowledge is treated as protected property, guild privilege, or strategic military resource and jealously guarded.

As a result, acquiring new spells may involve negotiation, patronage, favors, espionage, theft, or dangerous exploration as often as straightforward study.
#### Primed Formulae and Arcane Archives
A wizard may record any number of spells within his spellbooks, limited only by available time, materials, and access to magical knowledge. Successfully learned spells become part of the wizard’s personal **arcane archive** and need not be relearned once properly understood and recorded.

However, most wizards cannot maintain immediate familiarity with every spell they possess.

Through continual study and use, a wizard develops a smaller body of actively maintained spells known as **Primed Formulae**. These are spells whose notation, symbolic structure, and casting sequences remain readily accessible to the wizard’s trained mind, allowing them to be prepared through normal daily study.

The maximum number of Primed Formulae a wizard may maintain is normally determined by Intelligence, though specialist traditions, class features, magical research, and other exceptional circumstances may modify this limit.

Spells contained elsewhere within the wizard’s archive remain fully understood and may still be used, but they require additional review and re-familiarization before they may again be treated as Primed Formulae.

Re-priming a spell ordinarily requires one full uninterrupted day of study per spell level. When a wizard adds a new spell to his Primed Formulae beyond his normal limit, another primed spell of his choice is displaced back into the archive.

This limitation reflects not the total extent of a wizard’s magical knowledge, but the finite number of complex arcane formulae that can be maintained in a state of immediate familiarity and efficient preparation.
#### Starting Spellbooks
At the beginning of play, a wizard starts with a spellbook containing 3d4 1st-level spells, two of which must be *read magic* and *detect magic*. The DM may choose to simply assign the spells the character begins with, or may present the player with a selection of spells that they may attempt to learn.

These spells represent the character’s prior training, apprenticeship, institutional education, personal experimentation, or inherited magical knowledge.

Additional spells must be acquired during play.

### Spell Points and Arcane Preparation
Arcane spellcasting relies upon the controlled expenditure of mental focus, symbolic structure, and energetic capacity collectively represented through **Spell Points (SP)**.

Each time an arcane spell is cast, the caster expends a quantity of SP determined by the spell’s level and method of casting. More flexible or demanding forms of casting generally require greater expenditure, while carefully prepared magic may be shaped more efficiently.

Arcane practitioners divide their spellcasting methods into three broad approaches:

- **Fixed Magic**, consisting of deliberately prepared spells stabilized in advance,
- **Free Magic**, consisting of spontaneous casting drawn from a caster’s Primed Formulae,
- and **Ritual Magic**, consisting of prolonged casting performed directly from written formulae without prior preparation.

These approaches do not represent different forms of magic, but different methods of organizing and releasing arcane structure.
#### Fixed Magic
Fixed Magic consists of spells deliberately prepared through study, memorization, and energetic stabilization prior to casting.

During preparation, the wizard impresses carefully structured mnemonic and energetic patterns into the mind, partially shaping the spell in advance. The completed structure remains dormant until released through casting.

Because much of the spell’s complexity has already been stabilized beforehand, Fixed Magic is the most efficient and reliable form of arcane spellcasting.

A spell prepared as Fixed Magic:

- costs the normal SP value to cast,
- may be cast quickly using its normal casting time,
- may benefit from Overpowering,
- and may employ Prolonged Casting.

Once cast, the prepared structure collapses and must be prepared again before it can be used as Fixed Magic another time.
##### Arcane Preparation
Preparing Fixed Magic requires uninterrupted study of the caster’s spellbook or other arcane formula repository.

A wizard may prepare any spell contained within his Primed Formulae, provided he has sufficient available SP capacity to commit to the spell.

Preparing a spell requires:

-  sufficient unallocated spell points
- 10 minutes per spell level,
- access to the written formula,
- and a clear mind free from significant interruption or exhaustion.

Prepared spells remain fixed within the caster’s mind until cast. The Spell Points invested in its preparation remain committed and cannot be recovered or reassigned by any means short of casting the spell.

A wizard may prepare multiple copies of the same spell if desired, each preparation consuming its full SP cost separately.
##### Overpowering Fixed Magic
When preparing a Fixed spell, a wizard may choose to deliberately stabilize the formula at greater than normal intensity, increasing the spell’s effective caster level when eventually released. This process is known as **Overpowering**.

An Overpowered spell functions as though cast by a wizard of higher caster level for all level-dependent variables, to a maximum increase of +4 caster levels.

The additional SP cost is determined during preparation:

| Effective Caster Level Increase | Additional SP Cost   |
| ------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| +1 level                        | +50% normal SP cost  |
| +2 levels                       | +100% normal SP cost |
| +3 levels                       | +150% normal SP cost |
| +4 levels                       | +200% normal SP cost |

Fractions are always rounded upward.

Only spells with level-dependent variables may be Overpowered. The caster must declare any Overpowering at the time the spell is prepared; it cannot be applied spontaneously during casting.
##### Prolonged Casting
A wizard may deliberately cast certain spells more slowly in order to reduce the energetic strain required to release them safely. This process is known as **Prolonged Casting**.

Only spells with a normal casting time of less than 1 round may be cast in this manner.

When using Prolonged Casting:

- the spell’s SP cost is reduced by 25% (rounded upward),
- but its casting time increases from segments to full rounds on a one-for-one basis.

Thus:

- a spell with casting time 1 becomes 1 round,
- a spell with casting time 3 becomes 3 rounds,
- and so forth.

The caster must maintain concentration throughout the prolonged casting process. Damage, failed checks, incapacitation, or severe distraction may disrupt the spell before completion.
#### Free Magic
Free Magic allows a wizard to cast spells spontaneously without first preparing a specific spell formula.

Rather than stabilizing a complete spell in advance, the caster maintains only flexible arcane potential organized according to spell level and then shapes the final spell structure at the moment of casting.

This method provides far greater flexibility than Fixed Magic, but requires substantially greater concentration and energetic expenditure.

Free Magic may only be used to cast spells contained within the caster’s Primed Formulae.

A spell cast as Free Magic:

- costs additional SP beyond the spell’s normal value,
- cannot be Overpowered,
- and uses its normal casting time unless modified by other effects.

The caster must still allocate arcane capacity by spell level during preparation, even though specific spells are not chosen in advance.

Thus, a wizard preparing Free Magic decides how much potential energy is reserved for:

- 1st-level spells,
- 2nd-level spells,
- 3rd-level spells,
- and so forth,

without committing those reserves to particular spell formulae until casting occurs.

This reflects the greater instability and mental strain involved in shaping complex magical structures spontaneously.

As with Fixed Magic, Spell Points allocated to a particular spell level remain committed until expended through casting and cannot be freely reassigned without renewed preparation.
#### Ritual Magic
Ritual Magic allows an arcane practitioner to cast spells directly from written formulae without prior preparation and without Spell Point expenditure.

Rather than stabilizing the spell internally through memorization, the caster externalizes much of the spell’s structure through prolonged study, careful recitation, symbolic execution, and deliberate energetic shaping during the casting itself.

Ritual casting is extremely slow and vulnerable to interruption, but allows access to spells outside the caster’s currently primed repertoire.

Any spell contained within the caster’s arcane archive may potentially be cast as a ritual, provided the caster has the written formula at hand.

When a spell is cast as Ritual Magic, its casting time increases according to the following progression:

| Normal Casting Time | Ritual Casting Time         |
| ------------------- | --------------------------- |
| 'Segments'          | Equivalent number of rounds |
| Rounds              | Equivalent number of turns  |
| Turns               | Equivalent number of hours  |
| 1 hour or greater   | Cannot be ritual cast       |
Spells requiring ritual casting demand uninterrupted concentration and careful execution throughout the entire casting process. Damage, violent motion, environmental disruption, failed checks, or loss of access to the spell formula may ruin the ritual and force it to begin again.

Because of the greatly extended casting times involved, Ritual Magic is rarely practical during melee or similarly immediate dangers, though it may still be employed during siege warfare, magical preparation, exploration, travel, investigation, and other situations where sufficient time and protection are available.
### Signature Spells
Through obsessive study, repetition, and refinement, some wizards achieve extraordinary mastery over a single spell. Such formulae become known as **Signature Spells**: deeply internalized enchantments whose structures the caster can shape with unusual speed, efficiency, and precision.

Just as a warrior may devote himself to mastery of a favored weapon, a wizard may specialize in particular spells through focused practice and research. A wizard may possess up to one Signature Spell per spell level, provided he has sufficient proficiency slots, character points, or other resources required by the campaign.

Possessing a Signature Spell does not restrict the wizard’s broader magical repertoire. The caster continues to learn, prepare, and cast other spells normally.
#### Choosing a Signature Spell
A Signature Spell must be:

- a spell the wizard already knows,
- a spell recorded within his spellbook,
- and a spell of a level he is currently capable of casting.

A specialist wizard may only select Signature Spells belonging to his own specialized school or tradition unless the DM rules otherwise. Generalist mages may select Signature Spells freely from among any spells they can cast.

The cost required to designate a Signature Spell depends upon the spell’s level and the advancement system used in the campaign.
#### Mastering a Signature Spell
Developing a Signature Spell requires extensive experimentation, rehearsal, and refinement.

The wizard must devote:

- an available proficiency slot (weapon or non-weapon)
- a minimum of **two uninterrupted weeks** of study and practice,
- and **1d10 × 100 gp per spell level** in rare materials, laboratory expenses, testing components, instructional texts, and related research costs.

At the conclusion of this process, the wizard must successfully make a normal **Chance to Learn Spell** check.

Success indicates the spell has been mastered as a Signature Spell.

Failure indicates the wizard cannot achieve the level of instinctive precision necessary for true specialization. The spell may still be cast normally, but may never be used as a Signature Spell by that character. Any proficiency slots devoted for the specialization are **not** lost upon failure.

Any bonuses normally granted to magical research or spell learning apply to this check.
#### Benefits of a Signature Spell
A wizard casts his Signature Spell as though he were **two caster levels higher** for all level-dependent effects. If the spell possesses no meaningful level-based variables, the wizard instead may choose one of the following benefits each time the spell is cast:

- impose a **–2 penalty** to saving throws against the spell, or
- reduce the spell’s casting time by **3 initiative steps** (minimum 1).

In addition, the wizard gains exceptional efficiency in preparing the spell.

Whenever the wizard prepares Fixed Magic, he may prepare one casting of each Signature Spell he possesses **without expending its normal Spell Point cost**. This free preparation still counts towards any normal limits on number of spells prepared.

A Signature Spell prepared in this way may still be overpowered paying the normal cost of additional Spell Points for that spell.
#### Reputation and Signature Magic
Wizards known for Signature Spells often become closely associated with them in reputation, rumor, and legend.

A necromancer infamous for a particularly lethal _vampiric touch_, an invoker renowned for devastating _fireballs_, or a geomancer feared for intricate _symbol_ spells may become recognizable as much for their favored enchantments as for their personal names.

Many arcane duels, rivalries, apprenticeships, and magical schools trace their histories through the refinement and inheritance of such signature formulae.

## Divine Magic
Divine magic represents the channelling of arcane structure through external authority rather than internal formulation. Where arcane casters shape the Weave through studied formulae and practised control, divine casters petition higher powers for sanctioned access to its shaping forces.

A divine spell is not constructed so much as *granted*. Its final expression depends not only upon the caster’s intent, but also upon their faith, devotion, and alignment with the granting power, as well as that power’s willingness and capacity to express influence within a given domain or sphere.

Divine casters do not maintain spellbooks or arcane formulae. Instead they study and internalize scripture, liturgy, and doctrine, refining their understanding and discipline so that their mind and spirit remain receptive to divine response. Through prayer and observance they maintain alignment with the principles that allow spells to be received and shaped when needed.

### Spheres of Influence
While divine spells may be categorized according to the same philosophical, effectual, and thaumaturgical schools used to classify arcane magic, such distinctions are largely academic from the perspective of divine casters. Schools describe how a spell functions; spheres determine whether a divine authority may grant access to it at all.

Each deity, force, or philosophy exerts influence more strongly within certain domains of existence than others. These domains are known as Spheres of Influence, and they define the categories of spells normally available to the faithful. The degree of access a power grants to a sphere is further classified as either Major or Minor, reflecting how closely that sphere aligns with the nature, authority, and concerns of the granting power.

A divine caster may ordinarily invoke only spells that fall within a granted sphere of influence.
#### All
The All Sphere represents the most universal and broadly sanctioned expressions of divine authority. It includes effects that manifest directly through the presence, will, or recognition of a divine power, as well as a limited number of broadly applicable spells common to many faiths.

Typical spells include blessings, divine affirmation, interdiction, and other expressions of sanctioned authority, along with widely shared utilities such as removing curses or correcting spiritual or magical imbalance.

The Sphere of All exists primarily to ensure baseline functionality across all priesthoods and is commonly granted in at least Minor form to most divine casters.
#### Animal
The Animal Sphere governs the instincts, behaviours, and spiritual essences of beasts and non-sapient life. It includes the shaping, calming, enhancing, or coercing of animals and similar natural creatures.

It is commonly associated with nature deities, pastoral traditions, and powers concerned with stewardship of living ecosystems.
#### Astral
The Astral Sphere governs movement, passage, and communication across planar boundaries and liminal states of existence.

It includes spells of planar travel, extradimensional contact, and navigation between worlds. It is often associated with deities of travel, mystery, or cosmic order.
#### Chaos
The Chaos Sphere embodies instability, unpredictability, and the disruption of ordered outcomes. It expresses divine influence through distortion, confusion, misfortune, and uncontrolled variation.

It is typically associated with trickster powers, destructive entropy, and forces opposed to rigid structure or predictability.
#### Charm
The Charm Sphere governs influence over emotion, perception, loyalty, and interpersonal alignment. It affects attitudes, allegiances, and the subtle shaping of intent within intelligent beings.

It is commonly granted by deities of love, persuasion, deception, or social harmony, and is widely used by faiths engaged in mortal affairs.
#### Combat
The Combat Sphere contains spells intended for immediate personal conflict. It represents direct divine empowerment of the individual in violent or tactical encounters.

These effects are typically short-duration, direct, and focused on survival, offence, or immediate battlefield advantage.
#### Creation
The Creation Sphere governs the manifestation of matter, energy, or sustenance where none previously existed. It includes the production of basic necessities such as food, water, light, and simple material substances.

It is often associated with creator deities, providence, and powers concerned with sustaining life.
#### Divination
The Divination Sphere allows perception beyond ordinary limits, including insight into hidden truths, unseen forces, and uncertain outcomes.

It is the province of wisdom, foresight, and knowledge-seeking powers, and is often used to guide decision-making and interpret divine will.
#### Elemental
The Elemental Sphere governs the fundamental building blocks of physical reality: air, earth, fire, and water.

Divine powers with strong natural or primordial associations may grant access to one or more elemental aspects, though full access to all four is uncommon and usually indicative of broad natural authority.
#### Guardian
The Guardian Sphere focuses on the protection of specific sites, objects, or designated sacred boundaries. It emphasizes the defence of what is consecrated, claimed, or ritually bound.

Unlike broader protective magic, Guardian spells are anchored to place or object rather than individuals.
#### Healing
The Healing Sphere governs the restoration of physical and spiritual integrity. It includes the repair of injury, removal of affliction, and stabilization of life functions, but does not typically extend to the reversal of death or manipulation of life force itself.

It is among the most widely granted spheres across divine traditions.
#### Law
The Law Sphere expresses divine authority through order, structure, obligation, and collective stability. It reinforces hierarchy, obedience, and predictable outcome within social or metaphysical systems.

It is opposed conceptually to Chaos, though not necessarily aligned with morality in a simplistic sense.
#### Necromantic
The Necromantic Sphere governs life force, death, and the boundary between them. It includes the manipulation of vitality, the animation or dismissal of undead, and the restoration of life under exceptional circumstances.

It is one of the most tightly controlled and doctrinally sensitive spheres.
#### Numbers
The Numbers Sphere treats mathematical relationships as a reflection of underlying cosmic order. It allows insight, influence, or manipulation through numerical, proportional, or geometric principles.

It is typically associated with obscure or philosophical traditions rather than mainstream worship.
#### Plant
The Plant Sphere governs vegetative life in all its forms, from cultivated crops to wild forests. It includes growth, decay, shaping, and communication with plant life.

It is commonly granted to nature-focused deities and agricultural traditions.
#### Protection
The Protection Sphere governs the defence of individuals rather than locations. It provides wards, resistances, and enhancements that preserve life and integrity under threat.

It is widely granted to militant and protective faiths, especially those operating in hostile environments.
#### Summoning
The Summoning Sphere governs the calling of external entities into service, whether from nearby spiritual realms or distant extra-dimensional planes.

It includes both conjuration and dismissal, allowing divine authority over bound or summoned beings when properly invoked.
#### Sun
The Sun Sphere governs radiance, illumination, and the transformative power of light. It includes both practical and destructive applications of solar energy.

It is commonly associated with revelation, purity, and divine judgment.
#### Thought
The Thought Sphere governs cognition, perception, belief, and the interaction between mind and reality. It treats thought itself as an active force capable of shaping external outcomes.

It is typically associated with introspective, philosophical, or highly esoteric divine powers.
#### Traveller
The Traveller Sphere governs movement, endurance, and guidance through hostile or uncertain environments. It provides aid to those who journey, whether physically, spiritually, or politically.

It is commonly granted by deities of roads, pilgrimage, and exploration.
#### War
The War Sphere governs large-scale conflict, coordination, and the shaping of battlefield outcomes. Unlike the Combat Sphere, which focuses on individual encounters, War operates at the level of armies, formations, and strategic engagement.

It is granted by deities of conquest, defence, and martial authority.
#### Wards
The Wards Sphere governs barriers, deterrents, and persistent magical boundaries. It creates conditions that resist intrusion rather than directly empowering defenders.

It is often used to secure territory, sanctify boundaries, or establish controlled access.
### Holy Symbols and Religious Canon
Unlike arcane casters, who rely upon carefully preserved formulae and written magical structures, divine casters draw their power through faith, devotion, and spiritual alignment with the higher powers they serve.

Divine casters therefore do not require spellbooks or similar repositories of magical instruction in order to receive or cast spells. Their prayers, liturgies, meditations, and rites serve to align the caster with the divine will rather than to encode specific magical formulae.

A holy symbol is not ordinarily required for a divine caster to receive spells, though many priests employ one as a focus of devotion, authority, and spiritual identity. Certain spells, rites, and class abilities — most notably the turning of undead and similar manifestations of divine authority — may specifically require the presentation of an appropriate holy symbol.

Likewise, the sacred texts, doctrinal commentaries, hymns, ritual manuals, and canonical writings of a faith are not generally necessary for routine spellcasting. Such works instead serve as guides to theology, ritual observance, moral instruction, ecclesiastical law, and the performance of major ceremonies and religious rites.

Nevertheless, possession of a personal copy of the faith’s canon is widely regarded as an important symbol of devotion, scholarship, and spiritual maturity. A priest who neglects to acquire or maintain such texts after elevation beyond the rank of acolyte may invite quiet criticism, suspicion, or doubts regarding the seriousness of their faith and discipline.

### Divine Spell Points (DSP) and Prayer
Divine spellcasting relies upon the attention, favour, and granted authority bestowed by the priest’s deity, force, or philosophy, represented through Divine Spell Points (DSP).

Unlike arcane casters who have a fixed quantity of arcane spell points which are allocated towards fixed and free magic, expended by casting, renewed through rest, and then reallocated through study, divine casters pray to receive an allocation of DSP, with the quantity received varying from day to day according to divine favour and circumstance. The cost of each spell they invoke in a day is paid from this pool. While the favour granted by the divine is not entirely predictable, most faithful priests receive sufficient DSP each day to perform the ordinary obligations of their calling.

In order to receive an allocation of DSP the divine caster must spend 30 minutes in prayer or meditation for every 3 class levels to a maximum of 2 hours. The time spent in prayer need not be consecutive, but it must occur while able to engage in meaningful contemplation and/or devotional practice, such as while standing watch, engaging in light activity or travelling at a relaxed pace. The exact form these devotions take varies greatly between religions, philosophies, and other divine traditions.

Upon completion of the required time in prayer, and not more than once per day, the caster receives their base allocation of DSP as determined by their level. This represents stable divine obligation and consistent access to granted spheres.

Additional DSP may be granted each day based on the caster’s standing with their divine source and the circumstances surrounding their service.

Typical factors include:

* adherence to doctrine and ethos
* recent acts of service or alignment with divine goals
* presence at consecrated or sacred locations
* participation in rites, vows, or periods of devotion
* significant narrative or cosmological events

### Supplication (AKA Divine Ritual Casting)
Divine casters may perform supplication to petition their patron power for extraordinary intervention. Through extended prayer, offerings, and ritual observance, a priest may request the casting of spells that would not normally be available through their daily Divine Spell Point (DSP) allocation.

Supplication may be used to:

- cast a spell for which the priest has insufficient DSP,
- cast a spell outside the patron’s normal spheres of influence,
- or cast an overpowered version of a spell.

Supplication *cannot* be used to cast spells of a higher spell level than the caster could normally access through advancement and ability score limitations.

If the spell being supplicated is fundamentally opposed to the granting power’s nature or ethos, the supplication automatically fails. At the DM’s discretion, this may also result in a loss of favour with the granting power, reflecting strained alignment or doctrinal transgression.

#### Procedure
The caster must present an offering appropriate to the deity, force, or philosophy being invoked. The offering must be worth at least 50 gp, though greater offerings may improve the efficiency of the ritual.

The caster then spends uninterrupted rounds in prayer, meditation, or liturgical recitation. During this time, the spell is gradually “built” through divine response.

Each round of supplication generates 2 DSP toward the requested spell, modified as listed below:

|Condition|Modifier|
|-|-|
|Major offering (2000 gp+)|+3 DSP/Rd|
|Moderate offering (500-2000 gp)|+1 DSP/Rd|
|Significant Casting Location|+2 DSP/Rd|
|Favoured Casting Location|+1 DSP/Rd|
|Failure to observe normal rites and prayers|-1 DSP/Rd|
|Spell was cast within the last day|-3 DSP/Rd|

**Significant locations** are sites of established religious importance (temples, shrines, consecrated grounds).
**Favoured locations** are places symbolically aligned with one or more of the power’s domains, but not formally dedicated.

The ritual continues until the accumulated DSP equals the cost of the spell being requested.

##### Supplicated Spell costs
The cost of a supplicated spell is equal to its normal DSP cost, reduced by an amount equal to the caster’s level. The resulting cost cannot be reduced below twice the spell’s level.

If the spell is within the patron’s spheres of influence, use the DSP cost given in the first part of this section.
If the spell lies outside the patron’s spheres, it uses the Out-of-Sphere Cost below.

Out-of-Sphere Cost

|Spell Level|DSP Cost|

|3|30|

Supplication follows the normal rules for Concentration and Spell Disruption. If disrupted, the casting fails, all accumulated DSP are lost, and any offerings are consumed.

## Spellcasting Procedure
Spellcasting is a precise process requiring concentration and careful control of magical energy. All spells follow the same basic sequence:

1. the caster begins the spell,
2. required components are performed,
3. the spell is released at the end of its casting time.
#### Declaring Spellcasting
Outside of combat, a caster may begin casting a spell by stating which spell is being used and meeting all requirements for its casting. Casting begins immediately.

In combat, spells must be declared during the **Action Declaration Phase**. Casting begins at the start of the **Resolution Phase**, according to initiative order.

If a spell is begun outside combat and initiative is later required, the casting continues into combat. In such cases:

- spells with casting times of less than 1 round gain a **–3 initiative bonus**,
- spells with a casting time of 1 round use the spell’s level as an initiative modifier instead of resolving at end of round,
- spells with casting times greater than 1 round reduce remaining casting time by 1 round.

Once casting begins, the caster is committed to the spell. If casting is interrupted, the spell is lost and any Spell Points spent are wasted.
#### Casting Time
Each spell has a casting time that indicates how long it takes to complete.

If uninterrupted, the spell takes effect at the end of its casting time.

Casting times expressed as numbers are fast actions. In combat, these modify initiative. Outside combat, the DM determines order of resolution when necessary.

Casting times measured in **rounds, turns, or hours** require the full time to complete. Such spells do not require initiative rolls when used in combat and are resolved when their casting time ends.

As noted in the _Time and Movement_ rules:

- rounds are roughly minutes,
- turns are roughly tens of minutes,
- hours represent extended periods of time.
#### Components
Spells require one or more components:

- **Verbal (V)** — spoken words or sounds
- **Somatic (S)** — gestures or movements
- **Material (M)** — physical substances or items

If a required component cannot be performed, the spell fails.

Silence prevents verbal components. Restraint prevents somatic components. Absence of required materials prevents material components.

Material components are consumed only when the spell description states so.
#### Concentration and Spell Disruption
A spell requires concentration throughout its casting.

A spell is disrupted if the caster:

- chooses to abort the casting attempt,
- moves beyond the limits of shifting (see _Combat_),
- suffers damage before completion,
- fails a saving throw,
- is knocked prone, grappled, silenced, or otherwise impaired,
- loses required components,
- or is otherwise distracted or disrupted at the DM’s discretion.

A caster with the **Concentration** proficiency may resist disruption in some circumstances. A caster without this proficiency may attempt a Wisdom check (–2 penalty) when disruption is minor (typically 2 points of damage or less), with further penalties (usually –3 to –6) applied by the DM for greater interference.

If the check fails, the spell is lost. All Spell Points and time spent are wasted.

Unless otherwise stated, concentration is not required once the spell is completed.
#### Spell Completion and Release
When a spell’s casting time ends, its structure stabilizes and it is released immediately.

In combat, the caster may only shift position (see _Combat_) before the spell takes effect, unless otherwise stated in the spell description.

At this point:

- targets are chosen,
- ranges and areas are determined,
- saving throws are made,
- and the spell takes effect.

**Touch Spells** affect the caster rather than an external target at the moment of completion. In these cases, the spell energy is held by the caster, usually in the hand or hands.

When the spell is completed:

- the caster may move normally,
- and may deliver the spell through a touch attack as part of the same action,
- without requiring a separate initiative count.

This is not a separate attack but part of the spell’s resolution.

If no valid target is touched immediately, the spell remains charged for a limited duration (or until the end of the current round if not specified).

While charged:

- the caster may move normally,
- the spell discharges when the caster intentionally touches a valid target,
- accidental contact does not normally discharge the spell,
- some spells allow multiple discharges, while others end after a single use.

If the duration expires before discharge, the spell dissipates.
#### Line of Sight and Perception
A caster must normally be able to perceive the target or point of effect when the spell is released, unless the spell states otherwise.

Darkness, invisibility, concealment, blindness, smoke, or magical interference may prevent targeting at the DM’s discretion.

Some spells may function without direct sight.
#### Detection During Casting
Spellcasting may be noticeable. Depending on the spell and tradition, casting may produce words, gestures, light, sound, or other effects.

Characters with appropriate training or knowledge (such as Spellcraft or similar proficiency) may identify a spell during casting, especially if familiar with its form.

The DM determines what, if anything, can be identified before the spell is completed.
#### Ongoing Spell Effects
Most spells do not require concentration once completed and continue for their full duration unless dispelled or otherwise ended.

Some spells may be ended by the caster at will. To do so, the caster must be within range and able to perform any required dismissal.

Spells that require concentration end immediately if concentration is broken or becomes impossible.

## Mysticism
>“Most believe magic begins when something is done.  
>They are wrong.  
>Magic begins where something already is.”  
>— **Neruun the Listener**
### The Weave of Magic (expansion)
The most erudite scholars of the very nature of magic hypothesize that it is best understood as a weave. The warp of this weave is believed to emanate from the Outer Planes, aligning most strongly with what is known as divine magic and giving **shape** to the multiverse. The weft, in contrast, is thought to radiate from the Inner Planes, giving **substance** to the multiverse, and is most commonly associated with arcane magic.

Between these threads lie the interstices—the spaces formed by the crossing of warp and weft. These spaces are sometimes simply called **essence**. Unlike the threads themselves, essence is believed to arise naturally from the Prime Material, manifesting most commonly as the innate magics of the many creatures and races native to it. Essence provides **cohesion**, binding shape and substance together where they meet.

The magic of priests and wizards share much in common, for both rely upon plucking, twisting, and shaping the threads of the weave itself. To deliberately influence the tensions and spaces between those threads, however, is an entirely different and far rarer art.

This art is known as **Mysticism**.

**Note:** While many supernatural abilities are explained in this cosmology as expressions of Essence, only those abilities designated as **Powers** are resolved using the Mysticism rules presented here.
### What Is Mysticism (formerly Psionics)
>“Magic asks, ‘What can I make happen?’
>Mysticism asks, ‘What can I remain while it happens?’
>Those who fail to see the difference often master one art and misunderstand both.”

Mysticism represents the disciplined manipulation of the interstices of the Weave through inner cohesion. Rather than drawing upon external threads of power, mystics cultivate mastery over their own essence, shaping the spaces where magic does not reside, yet from which it arises.

Mystic practice is organized into structured **Disciplines** and expressed through specific **Practices** and **Devotions**, each reflecting a different way of exerting will, perception, or presence upon the unseen fabric that binds the multiverse together.
### Sidebar: Innate Magicks
Not all expressions of mystical power are learned or practised. Many creatures possess natural magical abilities that arise from the interstices of the Weave as a matter of their nature rather than through study, prayer, or discipline. These **innate magicks** are often instinctive, automatic, or inseparable from the creature itself.

Examples include:
- The breath weapon of a dragon, an expression of its inherent power rather than external spell energy
- A medusa’s petrifying gaze, an involuntary manifestation of supernatural influence
- Elementals channelling the raw forces of the Inner Planes through their own being

Cosmologically, such abilities are understood to be expressions of **Essence**—the same underlying substrate that mystics learn to influence through training and devotion. This shared origin explains why innate magics exist alongside arcane, divine, and mystical practices, but it does **not** imply a shared set of rules or resources.

Innate magicks are resolved using the normal mechanics provided in creature descriptions and do not draw upon Mystical Essence points, Practices, Devotions, or other elements of the Mysticism system unless explicitly stated otherwise.

Recognizing this shared origin can however assist DMs in adjudicating edge cases involving antimagic, resistance, or unusual interactions, but it does not change how such abilities function in play.
### Using Mysticism in Play
Mysticism is the study and application of **inner Essence**, the ambient force that flows in the spaces between warp and weft. Whereas wizards and priests manipulate the threads of the weave itself, mystics focus on cultivating their own cohesion and shaping the latent potential of the interstices. This approach allows for subtle, flexible, and often surprising effects—but requires discipline, concentration, and mastery over both self and surroundings.
#### Definitions
##### Disciplines
A **Discipline** represents a broad school of mystical practice, defining a mystic’s perspective and approach to manipulating Essence. Each Discipline provides a framework for developing **Practices** and **Devotions**, the specific techniques that a mystic can employ. While a mystic may study multiple Disciplines, mastery in even one requires years of focused training.

The core Disciplines are:
- **Discipline of the Inner Eye (aka Clairsentience):** Perceiving beyond ordinary senses, sensing danger, and uncovering hidden truths.
- **Discipline of the Vital Path (aka Psychometabolism):** Mastery of the body’s internal processes, including healing, enhancement, and resistance to harm.
- **Discipline of the Willing Mind (aka Telepathy):** Projection of thought, mental defence, emotional influence, and mind-to-mind contact.
- **Discipline of the Shaping Hand (aka Psychokinesis):** Direct manipulation of matter and energy through focused will.
- **Discipline of the Flowing Step (aka Psychoportation):** Movement beyond conventional limits, including teleportation, displacement, and spatial transposition.

These Discipline names are thematic placeholders; DMs may rename or flavour them to suit their campaign. Each represents a **distinct approach to manipulating Essence**.
##### Powers
Within a Discipline, a **Power** is a concrete mystical technique, a single effect or ability drawn from that Discipline. Powers are what a mystic performs in play: sensing a hidden foe, reinforcing an ally, or subtly influencing a target’s cohesion.

Powers are categorized based on their **scope and potency** as either:
- **Devotions (minor):** Focused, tactical techniques that affect a specific target or situation.
- **Practices (major):** Broad or potent techniques representing a deeper mastery of a Discipline.

Each Power consumes **ME Points (Mystical Essence Points)**, which measure the mystic’s capacity to consciously manipulate the interstices.
##### Devotions
A **Devotion** is a minor Power that expresses a specific, contained effect within a Discipline. Devotions are the everyday tools of a mystic: sensing danger, reinforcing an ally, subtly undermining a target’s cohesion, or performing other focused actions.
- A mystic may maintain several Devotions but must choose and apply them judiciously.
- Devotions can be offensive, defensive, or utility-focused.
- Devotions are generally quicker to activate and require fewer ME Points than Practices.

Through careful use of Devotions, a mystic can influence a battlefield or situation in ways fundamentally different from conventional magic, leveraging the subtle manipulation of Essence rather than raw force.
##### Practices (formerly Sciences)
A **Practice** is a major Power that embodies significant mastery over a Discipline. Practices represent effects that are broader, more powerful, or more enduring than Devotions.
- Practices can affect multiple targets, larger areas, or create lasting changes to the interstices of the Weave.
- They usually require greater concentration and more ME Points than Devotions.
- Practices are a hallmark of an experienced mystic and may form the foundation of a character’s distinctive style or signature abilities.

By mastering Practices, a mystic gains the capacity to shape situations on a larger scale, complementing the more precise effects of Devotions.
##### Transcendent Powers
A **Transcendent Power** is a specialized technique used to refine, intensify, or extend the effect of another Power — whether a Devotion or a Practice.
- It is never invoked independently; it always modifies an underlying Power.
- Transcendent Powers may increase range, duration, accuracy, or potency.
- Only one Transcendent Power may be applied to another Power unless explicitly stated.
- Using a Transcendent Power increases the ME cost of the underlying Power, reflecting the additional concentration and exertion required.

Using Transcendent Powers, mystics may push even minor Devotions to impressive effect or further amplify already powerful Practices, allowing for greater flexibility and customization of their mystical repertoire.
#### Mystical Essence
>“Essence is not spent because it is consumed.
>It is spent because the self must hold a shape it does not naturally prefer.”

**Mystical Essence (ME)** represents a creature’s capacity to consciously shape the interstices of the Weave through inner cohesion. All living creatures possess some measure of Essence, though only trained mystics can reliably direct it toward specific effects.

- ME is measured in **Mystical Essence Points (ME Points)**.
- Each Devotion has a listed ME Point cost, which must be paid to activate it.
- When a creature’s ME Points are reduced to 0, it cannot activate Devotions or other Essence-driven abilities until some ME is recovered.
#### Cohesion Bonus
>“A weak will is not one that doubts.  
>It is one that does not know where it ends.”  

The **Cohesion Bonus** represents a creature’s ability to _maintain a unified sense of self_ in the face of external mystical pressure. It measures how tightly bound a creature’s identity, will, experience, and awareness are—how difficult it is to intrude upon, fracture, or manipulate the space where its essence coheres.

Creatures with strong cohesion possess a stable internal structure that resists intrusion, whether through disciplined self-hood, alien nature, or sheer experiential weight. Creatures with weak cohesion are easier to influence, either because their identity is diffuse, instinct-driven, or fragmented.

The Cohesion Bonus is used in two primary ways:
- In the determination of **Mystical Armour Class (MAC)** for both mystics and non-mystics.
- By **non-mystics** when determining whether they resist, notice, or remain unaware of an attempt by a mystic or wild talent to force their mind open.

The Cohesion Bonus is calculated as:

> **Cohesion Bonus = HD/Level Modifier + Cohesion Modifier + Intelligence Modifier**

##### HD/Level Modifier

##### Intelligence Modifier
Intelligence contributes to cohesion because it reflects a creature’s **capacity for structured thought, self-awareness, and internal organization**. More intelligent beings possess a clearer internal model of self versus other, intention versus intrusion, and thought versus impulse. This makes subtle mystical influence harder to introduce without being detected or resisted.

Low intelligence does not imply vulnerability due to ignorance, but rather due to **lack of internal complexity**—there is simply less structure for an intruding will to push against. Conversely, highly intelligent creatures instinctively recognize and reject foreign patterns imposed upon their cognition, even if they have no training in Mysticism.

This modifier applies equally to trained and untrained creatures: intelligence strengthens cohesion regardless of whether or not a creature understands the source of the intrusion.

#### Sidebar: Why Mystical Essence Is Expended
Mystical Essence does not represent stored energy or external power. Instead, it reflects a mystic’s **capacity to maintain inner cohesion while exerting will upon the interstices of the Weave**.

Each Devotion requires the mystic to momentarily impose structure on spaces that resist form. This act strains the mystic’s sense of self, perception, and internal balance. As Devotions are used, the mystic’s cohesion becomes increasingly taxed—attention frays, focus wavers, and the ability to precisely shape Essence diminishes.

When a mystic’s ME is exhausted, it is not that power is absent, but that **the mystic can no longer reliably impose order upon the interstices without risking collapse or loss of control**.
#### Recovering Mystical Essence Points
Unlike users of Arcane or Divine magic, who must study or supplicate to prepare their spells, mystics restore their ability to shape Essence by reestablishing **inner balance**. This requires undisturbed rest or meditation, during which the mystic can centre themselves, realign mind and body, and disentangle and anchor their own Essence.

- Each full hour of uninterrupted rest restores **1/8th of the mystic’s total ME Points**, up to their maximum.
- Any disruption or activity—including using or maintaining a mystical Power—that is not sleep or meditation **resets the recovery clock**.
- For example, if a mystic sleeps for 90 minutes, keeps watch for a short period, and then sleeps for another 90 minutes, only **two full hours of uninterrupted rest** count toward ME recovery, not three.
#### Mystic Integrity (Formerly Closed and Open Minds)
>“No essence breaks cleanly.
>It bends, recoils, and remembers the pressure long after the hand is gone.”  

The essence embodied in individual creatures is **elastic**—it can be stretched, twisted, and displaced, but it is never inert. The more tension that is applied to a creature’s essence, the more strongly it resists, seeking to return to its intrinsic form.

This quality is known as **Mystic Integrity**. It describes the current state of a creature’s internal cohesion under mystical pressures, and determines how readily further mystical influence can be applied.

Mystic Integrity is not a measure of damage, nor does it represent injury or loss of self. Instead, it reflects how much **strain** has been imposed upon a creature’s essence and how close that essence is to slipping out of its natural alignment.
#### States of Mystic Integrity
A creature’s Mystic Integrity exists in one of the following states:
- **Intact**
    This is a creature's natural state, it's essence fully aligned and self-righting. The mystic may attempt to impose upon a target's essence - either gently or forcefully - but cannot use any power against a creature that does not consciously and willingly allow it.
- **Strained**  
    The creature’s essence has been stretched or displaced by sustained or focused mystical pressure. While not broken, its unity is compromised. The mystic has engaged the target's essence and may use devotions that do not cause direct harm (i.e. damage) to the creature. 
- **Breached**  
    The creature’s essence has been forced out of alignment. Its self-righting tension is temporarily overwhelmed, allowing direct mystical influence to flow at its full strength. At this stage, Devotions and Practices may be applied.
### Integrity, Elastic Rebound, and Recovery
>"I have watched spells shatter mountains and prayers silence gods. I have also watched men break beneath a whisper they did not understand. Essence does not care what shaped the storm—only whether you can stand when it passes."

Mystic Integrity is inherently temporary. Essence is elastic: when mystical pressure is released, a creature’s essence naturally seeks to reassert its intrinsic form and return to alignment.

How and how quickly this occurs depends on **awareness**, **cohesion**, and whether mystical pressure is still being actively applied.
#### Two Modes of Recovery
Recovery from mystical influence occurs in one of two distinct modes:
- **Natural Elastic Recovery (Mystic undetected)**  
    Slow, passive, and involuntary. Occurs only while the target remains unaware of the intrusion.
- **Active Re-Centring (Mystic detected)**  
    Fast, forceful, and contested. Occurs only once the target becomes aware that its essence is under attack.

A creature may not attempt saving throws to recover Mystic Integrity unless it is **aware** of the intrusion, unless otherwise stated.
### Natural Elastic Recovery (Undetected)
While mystical influence remains **undetected**, a creature’s essence recovers on its own once pressure is released. No saving throws are made.

Natural recovery begins when **all** of the following are true:
- The mystic has not expended Mystical Essence on that target for the duration of the recovery interval
- No Devotion or Practice is currently being actively maintained on the target
- The mystic is no longer within effective range, or is no longer directing influence toward the target

Natural recovery occurs automatically over time, measured from the last moment mystical pressure was applied.

| Current Integrity State | Natural Recovery Time | Result                   |
| ----------------------- | --------------------- | ------------------------ |
| **Strained**            | 1d10 minutes          | Returns to **Intact**    |
| **Breached**            | 1d20 minutes          | Improves to **Strained** |

Recovery from **Breached** to **Intact** requires completing both stages.
#### Cohesion and Natural Recovery
The creature’s Cohesion Modifier alters natural recovery time:
- **Diffuse or Unstable Cohesion:** ×2 recovery time
- **Rigid or Alien Cohesion:** ×½ recovery time (minimum 1 minute)

Rest, calm conditions, and emotional stability encourage elastic rebound. Stress, fear, or repeated interference may delay recovery at the DM’s discretion.

If mystical pressure is reapplied at any point—by expending ME on the target or reestablishing active influence—natural recovery halts or resets.
#### Active Re-Centering (Detected)
Once a creature becomes **aware** that its essence is being manipulated—most commonly when its **Detection Score reaches 4**—natural recovery immediately ceases. The struggle for cohesion becomes conscious and forceful.

From this point onward, recovery is resolved through **saving throws**, made as part of end-of-round resolution.
#### From Strained
A creature whose integrity is **Strained** and who is aware of the intrusion may attempt to re-centre itself at the end of each round:
- **Saving Throw:** vs. paralyzation
- **Penalty:**
    - No penalty if no powers were successfully used against the target that round
    - **–4 penalty** if a mystical power was successfully used against the target that round

On a successful save, the creature’s Mystic Integrity returns to **Intact**, all active Powers affecting it immediately cease, and access is broken.
#### From Breached
A creature whose integrity is **Breached** cannot immediately attempt to recover.
- **Delay:** The creature must endure **1d4+1 rounds** before it may attempt to re-center
- During this delay, the mystic may continue to exploit the breach normally

After the delay has elapsed, the creature may attempt to re-centre at the end of each round:
- **Saving Throw:** vs. paralyzation
- **Penalty:** **–3**

On a successful save:
- Mystic Integrity improves from **Breached** to **Strained**
- All active Practices immediately cease
### Ending Influence and Contact
Whenever Mystic Integrity returns to **Intact**, by any means:
- All Devotions and Practices currently affecting the creature immediately cease
- The mystic's access to the target is broken
- Re-establishing influence requires the mystic to once again compromise the target’s essence

Unless otherwise stated, Mystic Integrity cannot be permanently damaged by Devotions alone.
### Sidebar: Narrative Implications
A creature whose integrity is strained or breached but who remains unaware of the intrusion may experience unease, intrusive thoughts, déjà vu, emotional distortion, or a vague sense of being observed.

If integrity recovers before an imposed course of action is completed, the creature may “awaken” to find itself displaced in space, time, or intent—often with no clear memory of what compelled the action, only the unsettling certainty that something else once guided their will.
### Compromising a Target (formerly Psionic Combat)
>“To touch another’s essence is not violence.
>But neither is it harmless.” 

Mystics automatically receive **Imposition** as a bonus non-weapon proficiency when created. This training grants them access to mystic attack forms used to compromise a target’s essence, and mystic defences used to resist or deflect the impositions of others.

Wild Talents, in contrast, must expend a non-weapon proficiency slot to acquire Imposition, and cannot use their mystical powers until they do so (ordinarily at character creation or upon first manifesting as a wild talent). Additionally, a wild talent must expend non-weapon proficiency slots to learn new attack forms, and is limited in the total number of attack forms and defences they may know (see _Wild Talents_ in the _Class_ chapter).

Unless a target is conscious and willing, a mystic must first **engage and compromise the target’s essence** before they can impose their will or directly influence that creature’s cohesion. This engagement may be subtle or overt, depending on the relative cohesion, intelligence, and skill of both the mystic and the target.
### Using Imposition
When attempted in combat, the mystic must declare their intent to use **Imposition** to establish **Access** or to make a mystic attack before initiative is rolled, specifying the target. In any round where Imposition is thus used, the mystic is limited to **half normal movement** and must maintain focus until their initiative count. As with spellcasting, taking damage before the mystic’s initiative count disrupts this focus, causing the attempt to fail and expending **1 ME**.

A **mystic defence**, as detailed later in this section, must also be declared before initiative is rolled. A defence may be used on its own or in addition to any other action except casting a spell. Unlike Access and attacks, mystic defences do not require focus, cannot be disrupted, and do not impair movement.

**N.B.** If Imposition is used outside of combat, the same declaration process is followed. Initiative rolls are not automatically required, but may be used at the DM’s discretion to resolve matters of timing or interference. When initiative is not used, the DM should still consider the passage of time within the scene for the purposes of determining the cost of maintaining powers and the recovery of Mystic Integrity.

The first round Imposition is used against a target is always an attempt to establish **Access**. This is resolved on the mystic’s initiative count with a normal attack roll using their **MTHAC0** against the target’s **MAC**, and costs **2 ME** whether or not it is successful. In subsequent rounds, the mystic must declare on their initiative count which specific mystic attack or power they are using against the target; these are resolved using the same attack roll.

A mystic that has successfully accessed, strained, or breached a target must expend a minimum of **1 ME per round** to maintain that contact. Using any mystic attack or power against the target satisfies this requirement. If the mystic wishes to maintain contact while performing non-mystical actions, or while acting against other targets, they may instead expend **1 ME** to do so; no additional attack roll is required, and movement restrictions and focus requirements do not apply. If contact is not maintained, the target’s Mystic Integrity begins to recover normally.

A target that is itself a mystic or wild talent automatically senses when it has been targeted by a mystical attack or power and knows the source. A non-mystic does not automatically become aware and must instead detect the intrusion as described below.
#### Detecting Imposition (Non-Mystics)
>“Most do not notice the wind until something moves against it.”  

Until a non-mystic becomes aware of an attempt to influence them, an opposing detection roll is made **each time a mystical attack roll is made** against them. Maintaining contact that has already been established, or sustaining powers already in effect, does not affect detection.

**1d20 + Cohesion Bonus** (success on 17+)

Depending on the outcome of both rolls (the attack and the detection), the target’s **Detection Score** increases as shown below. Detection Score is cumulative.

| Result of Rolls                  | Detection Score Increase | Effect When Accumulated Detection Score Reaches… |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Mystic succeeds, Target fails    | +0                       | 1 – General unease or disquiet                   |
| Mystic succeeds, Target succeeds | +2                       | 2 – Vague sense of external influence            |
| Mystic fails, Target succeeds    | +4                       | 3 – Suspects attack, but not the source          |
| Mystic fails, Target fails       | +1                       | 4 – Fully aware of the attack and attacker       |
Once awareness is achieved, all ongoing mystical influence is considered detected for the purposes of recovery and resistance.
### Access, Strain, and Breach
Regardless of detection, the **first successful mystic attack** grants the mystic **access to the target’s essence**. At this stage, the target’s Mystic Integrity remains **Intact**.

On subsequent successful attacks, the mystic may strain or immediately breach the target’s essence, depending on the attack form used.

- While a target’s integrity is **Strained**, the mystic may:
    - Use Devotions that do not cause direct harm, or
    - Continue making mystic attacks in an attempt to achieve a breach.
- Once a **Breach** is achieved, the mystic may:
    - Use any Devotion or Practice they know against the target, or
    - Continue using mystic attacks to inflict the damage or additional effects described in the attack form.
#### Sidebar: Detecting Mystical Influence
The detection rules for non-mystics presented in _Compromising a Target_ are intentionally broad. They provide a framework for adjudicating awareness, not a rigid procedure. The DM should freely adjust outcomes based on circumstance, context, and narrative logic.

A distracted, exhausted, or inebriated target may be slower to notice that their essence is being strained, while a wary individual—especially one who knows, by rumour or experience, that a mystic is nearby—may recognize the signs of intrusion far more quickly. Emotional state, environment, and recent events should all inform how detection manifests.

It is likewise left to the DM’s discretion whether, and under what conditions, a mystic or wild talent can detect **another creature** being targeted by mystical influence. Factors to consider include proximity, the scale or violence of the effect, and whether the observing mystic is actively manipulating Essence themselves. A mystic deeply engaged in shaping the interstices may be more sensitive to disturbances caused by others.

Deliberately, these rules do not dictate what happens once an attempt at influence is detected. Awareness does not require confrontation. A target might grow uneasy, suspicious, or hostile—or they might conceal their awareness, allowing the mystic to believe their efforts remain unnoticed, perhaps setting the stage for future consequences.

If mystics are expected to appear as adversaries or recurring influences, the DM may find it useful to track the **Cohesion Bonus** of non-mystic PCs (or at least their Intelligence score and level). This allows the DM to request a simple d20 roll—or make one secretly—and describe the resulting sensations or intuitions without immediately revealing that a mystical attack is occurring.
## Mystic Attack Forms (Imposition)
### Spike of Intent
>ME Cost: 3
>Range: 90 feet
>Attack Modifier: +2

The mystic compresses their will into a single, sharply defined impulse and drives it toward the target’s essence. Rather than battering or destabilizing the target, the Spike seeks seams—moments of doubt, lapses of focus, or tiny discontinuities in self-hood—and exploits them with surgical exactness. To the untrained mind, the intrusion feels like a fleeting thought that was never one’s own.

**Imposition Effects:**
- reduces detection score increase by 1 (minimum 0).
- on a successful attack a creature's mystic integrity erodes by one step, (intact >  strained; strained > breached)
- prior to the attack roll the mystic may spend an additional 2 ME to enhance this attack against an intact mind. If the attack roll exceeds the target's MAC by 5 or more, the target's essence is immediately breached.

**Effect after Breach**:
Spike of Intent becomes a weapon of control instead of access.
- On a successful hit, the mystic may choose to:
    - Inflict 1d4 mystical damage **or**
    - suppress the target's next attack
### Identity Fracture  
>ME Cost: 4
>Range: 60 feet
>Attack Modifier: -1

The mystic hammers the target’s sense of self with conflicting impulses, memories, and assertions of being. Rather than finding a weakness, this assault attempts to **force** one into existence—splintering identity through sheer pressure. The sensation is unmistakable: confusion, emotional whiplash, and an alien sense of internal collision.

**Imposition Effects:**
- on a successful attack a creature's mystic integrity erodes by one step, (intact >  strained; strained > breached)
- prior to the attack roll the mystic may spend an additional 2 ME to remove the penalty to attack **or** an additional 4 ME to enhance this attack against an intact mind. In the latter case, if the attack roll succeeds, roll 1d100 and if result is 50 or less, the target's essence is immediately breached.

**Effect after Breach:**
Identity Fracture causes internal backlash.
- On a successful hit, the mystic may choose to:
	- Inflict 1d6 mystical damage **or**
	- Impose a -2 penalty to the target's cohesion bonus until the end of the next round. (This penalty may not stack with itself.)
### Seed of Discord
>ME Cost: 2
>Range: 120 feet
>Attack Modifier: nil

Rather than attacking directly, the mystic plants a resonant dissonance within the target’s essence—a contradiction that does not belong. Left alone, it spreads, tugging gently but persistently at the target’s internal balance. The target may never realize the thought was foreign.

**Imposition Effects:**
 - no detection score increase regardless of outcome
 - on a successful attack a creature's mystic integrity becomes strained.
 - prior to the attack roll the mystic may choose to spend an additional 5 ME. The target's current cumulative detection score is reduced by 1 if the mystic's attack roll succeeds. If the target is already strained the mystic may choose to forego the preceding effect and instead obtain a limited breach, allowing them to treat the target *as though* they had obtained a breach for the purpose of using practices against the target, for 1d3+1 rounds. This limited breach does not alter the target’s actual Mystic Integrity and does not enable post-breach effects of mystic attack forms.

**Effect after Breach:**
The Seed feeds on instability.
- On a successful hit, the mystic chooses one of the following:
    - The target’s ability to return to an intact state is weakened, suffering a -2 penalty to any rolls to centre itself; **or**,
    - The next power used by the mystic against the target costs 1 less ME (minimum 1) to initiate and maintain.
### Disjunction Wave
> ME Cost: 5
> Range: 30 feet
> Attack modifier: nil

The mystic releases a broad surge of will that violently disrupts the alignment between the target’s essence and its intrinsic form. This is not subtle manipulation—it is a forced realignment that leaves psychic echoes in its wake. Even witnesses may feel a momentary pressure behind the eyes.

**Imposition Effects:**
- Detection Score increase is 2 greater than normal 
- On a successful attack a creature's mystic integrity is breached,
- prior to the attack roll the mystic may spend 1 additional ME to impose a 1 point penalty to the target's next 2 attack or saving throw rolls.

**Effect after Breach:**
Disjunction Wave inflicts raw mystical trauma.

- a successful hit deals 1d12 mystical damage; if the damage roll is 10 or higher the target also suffers a knockdown unless immune to such effects.
### Spirit Sunder
>ME Cost: 3
>Range: 90 feet
>Attack Modifier: +1

The mystic applies steady, deliberate pressure along the bonds that hold the target’s essence together—not tearing them apart, but **cutting** them apart over time. Each strike weakens the internal ties until resistance fails. Victims often describe a sense of being slowly pulled out of alignment.

**Imposition Effects:**
- on a successful attack a creature's mystic integrity erodes by one step, (intact >  strained; strained > breached)
- the target suffers a -1 penalty on the next saving throw it must make during the current encounter. This effect is cumulative to a maximum penalty of -3.

**Effect after Breach:**
Spirit Sunder excels at maintaining dominance.
- On a successful hit the mystic may choose one of the following effects:
	- The target is prevented from returning to an intact state for 1d4+1 rounds **or**
	- The cost of maintaining powers used against the target is reduced by 2 (minimum 1) for 1d8+1 rounds

## Mystic Defence Forms (Imposition)
There are 5 defences a mystic or wild talent may learn. Each is particularly effective against certain forms of imposition and vulnerable to others. These interactions are resolved using the Attack–Defence Matrix which is presented after the descriptions of the defences with the value from the matrix applied as a modifier to the target's MAC; no defence is universally effective. 

Activating a mystic defence requires no concentration and cannot be disrupted; its only limitation is the expenditure of Mystical Essence and the requirement to declare it before initiative is rolled. The cost of each defence is included in the attack-defence matrix and must be paid each round the defence is active.
### Bastion of Reason
The mystic reinforces their sense of self through rigid internal structure: ordered thought, firm memory, and deliberate assertion of identity. Each impulse is catalogued, each perception grounded in logic and continuity. Intrusive forces find no purchase, sliding across an inner surface too well-defined to deform.

To an outside observer, the mystic appears calm, even detached. Within, however, the mind has become a fortified architecture—unyielding, uncompromising, and resistant to sudden disruption.

Bastion of Reason excels at withstanding **violent or overwhelming intrusions**, but struggles against influences that do not seek to break structure so much as bypass it entirely.
### Veil of Stillness
The mystic does not resist intrusion so much as **cease to respond to it**. Thought slows. Emotion quiets. The interstices around the self grow placid, offering no turbulence for external influence to catch upon.

Incoming pressure disperses into calm inertia, its force blunted by the absence of reaction. The mystic’s presence feels muted, as though partially withdrawn from the moment.

Veil of Stillness is most effective against **sustained pressure and grinding influence**, but can be overwhelmed by sudden, forceful disruptions that demand immediate response.
### Shroud of Emptiness
The mystic hollows the space around their essence, allowing nothing of substance to remain. There is no resistance, no structure, no surface upon which intrusion may land—only absence. Influences aimed at shaping thought or planting contradiction find nothing to attach to and fade without consequence.

To others, the mystic may seem distant, unfocused, or strangely unreadable, as though something expected simply is not there.

Shroud of Emptiness confounds **subtle, insinuating influences**, but offers little protection against assaults that generate their own pressure rather than relying on resonance.
### Aegis of Reflection
The mystic establishes a reactive boundary within the interstices, tuned to mirror and redirect incoming intent. Precision intrusions rebound along their own trajectory, their force dissipated or turned aside by the symmetry of the defense.

The mystic remains alert and responsive, every attempted incursion answered in kind—not resisted, but answered.

Aegis of Reflection is particularly effective against **focused, deliberate intrusions**, but loses efficacy when faced with diffuse or relentless pressure that offers no clear vector to turn aside.
### Pillar of Will
The mystic anchors themselves in conviction—emotion, belief, and intent fused into a single, immovable axis. Conflicting impulses collide with this core and fracture, unable to dislodge the mystic from their chosen alignment.

The effect is palpable. The mystic’s presence feels weighty, resolute, and difficult to sway, as though their very being insists upon remaining whole.

Pillar of Will stands firm against **attacks that seek to destabilize identity or induce internal conflict**, but is less effective against influences that erode cohesion gradually rather than confronting it directly.

## Mystical Limitations
A mystic may only attempt to establish **Access** to a creature whose presence they can currently perceive, either through mundane senses (sight, hearing, touch, etc.) or with mystical or magical assistance to those senses. (scrying, *Inner Eye*, a crystal ball, etc). Knowledge, belief, or familiarity with a creature does not constitute perception.

In normal conditions, once Access has been successfully established, and as long as they have sufficient ME to expend, the mystic may continue to target that creature with mystic attacks and powers (within their respective ranges, and for powers only those with the prerequisite of 'access') until contact is broken or the target’s Mystic Integrity fully recovers. (see *Integrity, Elastic Rebound, and Recovery*). 

Some materials exude their own effects upon the essence and obstruct continued contact with a target. These include lead or iron (at least 1" thick), obsidian or adamantine (at least 2" thick), stone (at least 2' thick). These materials also include planar and dimensional boundaries - if the mystic and their target come to be on different planes or extra-dimensional spaces, access is lost 

Powers with the range "touch" require physical contact between the mystic and the target - no mystic or magical augmentation may extend this range. These powers require a normal attack roll using THAC0 vs AC, **not** MTHAC0 and MAC.

Powers that do no have the prerequisite of "access" require direct perception of the target by the mystic.
#### Limitations on Mystic Powers in a Round
Within a single round, and assuming the individual ME costs of each can be paid, the following can be done:

1. Use a mystical defence for the round (possibly extending it to others), **and**  
2. Maintain powers that are already active, **and**  
3. Maintain contact (Access/Strain/Breach) with any number of targets, **and**  
4. Do one of the following:  
	1. Activate **one new power** against a Breached Mind, **or**  
	2. Make as many mystical attacks as allowed by the character's current level, **or**
	3. Perform any other action the character is capable of, **except casting a spell**
## Interaction of Mysticism and Traditional Magicks
>“The essence of the world is not a melody but the air itself—ever-present, vibrating, and alive. Arcane and divine magic impose music upon that air: structured, deliberate, and external. The notes may momentarily shape how the air moves, but they cannot still it, bind it, or make it follow the score. When the music ends, the air continues as it always has.”

As explained at the beginning of this chapter, the essence which mystics influence is fundamentally different from the threads of the Weave manipulated by spellcasters. The former originates in and permeates the Prime Material itself, while the latter are of extra-planar origin. Where they intersect, **each affects the other**, but never in equal measure and rarely in symmetrical ways.

Magic imposes structure upon essence, shaping how it may be perceived, bounded, or expressed. Mysticism, in turn, alters the _medium_ through which magic manifests, subtly distorting, resisting, or recontextualizing spell effects without unraveling their structure outright. As a result, interactions between magic and mysticism are often **partial, indirect, or transient**, and frequently defy purely magical expectations.
### Governing Principles
When adjudicating interactions between magic and mysticism, consider which of the following domains are being acted upon, and in which direction:
- **Perception:** Magic may reveal or obscure mystic influence; mysticism may alter what magic perceives or how information is framed.
- **Continuity:** Magic may impose boundaries, containment, or planar separation; mysticism may strain, bypass, or lose cohesion across such divisions.
- **Embodiment:** Magic may free, bind, or transform the body; mysticism may pressure or override the body’s role as an anchor for effects.
- **Cognition:** Magic may impose mental states or compulsions; mysticism may destabilize, resist, or reinterpret those states from within.

If an effect does not meaningfully engage one of these domains, it is unlikely to strongly interact with the other discipline, even if the outcomes appear superficially similar.
### Detection, Dispelling, and Separation
Spells that detect, dispel, suppress, or negate magic do not directly affect mystical powers, Devotions, Mystic Integrity, or the expenditure of Mystical Essence unless a spell explicitly states otherwise. _Detect magic_ reveals nothing unusual about a creature under mystical influence, nor does _dispel magic_ end or interrupt a Devotion or Practice. Likewise, mystical perception does not automatically register spellcasting, magical effects, or prepared spells.

This separation holds even when a spell and a mystical power produce similar outcomes. A magically sealed door and a door held shut through mysticism are secured by fundamentally different means; effects that undo one do not necessarily affect the other.

That said, magic can still **interrupt mysticism indirectly**. If a spell renders a target imperceptible, removes it from the mystic’s plane, or otherwise breaks continuity as defined under _Mystical Limitations_, Access is lost and Mystic Integrity recovers normally.
### Perception, Illusion, and Interference
Although magic cannot directly command essence, it can significantly alter the conditions under which a mystic operates. Spells that manipulate perception, environment, or cognition may interfere with mystical powers by obscuring targets, distorting awareness, or destabilizing cohesion.

Illusions that alter light, sound, or other sensory input affect mystical powers normally if those powers rely on perception or the extension of ordinary senses. However, when a mystic successfully establishes Access, strains Integrity, or applies a Devotion against a target affected by an illusion, the resulting feedback grants the mystic an immediate saving throw vs. spells to recognize the deception. On a success, the illusion no longer deceives that mystic, though it may continue to affect others.

Spells that directly affect the mind—such as charms, compulsions, or phantasms—do not block mystical influence, but they may weaken or distort it. At the DM’s discretion, such effects may impose temporary penalties to Cohesion, delay recovery to an Intact state, or otherwise influence how Mystic Integrity behaves while the spell persists.
### Common Magical Interactions
The following examples are illustrative rather than exhaustive:

| Category                   | Example Spells                      | Typical Interaction                                     |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Magical Detection**      | _detect magic, misdirection_        | Do not detect mysticism                                 |
| **Perceptual Revelation**  | _detect invisibility, detect charm_ | Reveal mystic effects if consequences are perceptible   |
| **Cognitive Wards**        | _mind blank, nondetection_          | Obscure detection; mysticism may still strain or breach |
| **Illusions (sensory)**    | _major image_                       | Affect perception; mystic insight may pierce            |
| **Phantasms (mental)**     | _phantasmal killer_                 | Mystics gain heightened resistance                      |
| **Containment / Barriers** | _forbiddance, resilient sphere_     | Block or sever Access                                   |
| **Embodiment Effects**     | _free action, protection from evil_ | Negate bodily control; integrity effects may persist    |
### Adjudication Examples
**Mystic Strain vs. Magical Compulsion**  
A creature under _suggestion_ is simultaneously subjected to mystic Strain. The spell remains intact, but the pressure introduces hesitation, reinterpretation, or resistance—effects emerging from strained cohesion rather than spell failure.

**Mind Blank vs. Imposition**  
_Mind blank_ prevents magical detection and blocks many enchantments. It does not prevent Access or Integrity pressure, but interferes with precision and awareness. Mystic attacks against such a target may require greater effort to achieve Breach.

**Antimagic Fields vs. Mystic Influence**  
An antimagic field suppresses spells but does not dispel existing mystic influence. If the field disrupts perception or continuity, Access may be lost as a consequence of separation, not negation.

**Free Action vs. Psychokinesis**  
_Free action_ negates restraints on the body, whether magical or mystic. Any ongoing strain on identity or cohesion remains until it naturally recovers or is actively resisted.
### Magic That Mimics Mysticism
Many spells replicate effects resembling mystical powers—sensing thoughts, altering perception, moving objects, or traversing space. These effects remain magical in nature and are resolved entirely using the rules for spells. Mystic defenses, Integrity states, and Imposition do not apply unless a spell or power explicitly states otherwise.

Similarity of outcome does not imply similarity of cause.
### Spells of Special Note
The following spells interact with mysticism in notable ways:
- **Detect Magic:** Never detects mystic effects. 
- **Globe of Invulnerability / Minor Globe:** Do not block mysticism.
- **Spell Immunity:** Does not confer immunity to mysticism.
- **Forbiddance:** Prevents establishing or maintaining Access across its boundary.
- **Otiluke’s Resilient Sphere:** Blocks mystic contact and powers.
- **Trap the Soul:** A mystic so trapped cannot use mystic powers.
- **Protection from Evil:** Grants modest resistance against domination.
- **Mind Blank:** Obscures detection and precision but does not negate Imposition.
- **ESP:** Mystics gain a +2 bonus on saving throws to resist.

Unless otherwise stated, mystics gain a +2 bonus on saving throws against enchantment or charm spells.
### Sidebar: Final Adjudication Guidance
When magic and mysticism interact in unclear ways, apply the following rule of thumb:
- **Spells determine what happens in the world.**
- **Mysticism determines how well a creature holds itself together while it happens.**

Magic may obscure, isolate, or constrain. Mysticism may resist, distort, or endure. Neither fully cancels the other unless a rule explicitly says so—and when they collide, the result is almost always friction rather than negation.

>“Spells change the world. Mysticism changes how you endure it.
>Many confuse the two, and many more perish because of that confusion.
>If you would walk the path of essence, remember this: power is not found in control, but in coherence. When all else is stripped away, what remains is not the spell that was cast, but the self that survived it.” 
— **Neruun the Listener**
